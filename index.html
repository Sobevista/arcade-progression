ARCADE PROGRESSION

Re-deriving the evolution of video games, in order, from the constraints up.

Not tribute builds. We work out what the original designers must have spent a long time figuring out — what machine they had, what it physically couldn't do, and what that forced them to invent — then build it ourselves and keep the lessons. The games are the vehicle. The deliverable is the trove:

  • INVARIANTS.md — laws, each carrying the failure that bought it
  • UX_DECISIONS.md — choices, tradeoffs priced, each with a re-pick trigger
  • ARCHAEOLOGY.md — history: what they were up against, and which constraints we keep on purpose

Example of what falls out: the invaders speeding up as you kill them is the most famous thing about Space Invaders — and it isn't in the source code. It was an Intel 8080 bottleneck. The genre's defining feature was never designed, which means on modern hardware the game has no difficulty curve at all unless you put one there deliberately.

The rungs

  • 01 Pong 1972 SEPARATE REPO paddle and ball physics, scoring — Python/pygame
  • 02 Invaders 1978 PLAY formation, tempo ramp, destructible terrain — TI-99/4A palette
  • 03 Breakout 1976 PLAY reflection angles, tile grid, an authored difficulty curve — and no CPU at all, pure TTL logic
  • 04 Galaga 1981 PLAY entry-flight choreography, dive AI, capture and rescue, challenging stages — the first rung with sprite hardware
  • 05a Hunt the Wumpus 1980 PLAY the homage machine itself — deduction as a genre: clues, inference, one arrow. Turn-based, no reflexes
  • 05b Parsec 1982 PLAY the TI's flagship — the ladder's first scrolling world, fuel, laser heat, refuel tunnel, and speech as an information channel
  • 06a Munch Man 1982 PLAY the lawsuit-forced inversion — FILL the maze with a chain instead of eating dots, and the energizer speeds YOU up. Four Hoonos with personalities
  • 06b Anteater 1983 PLAY you are the ant — the tunnels you dig persist, and the anteaters use them. Delayed-fuse eggs aimed by retreating; rocks fall when undermined
  • 07 Deliverance 1985 tech PLAY the NES rung — SMB physics transcribed from the disassembly (subpixel fixed point, forward-only camera), retold as the Exodus: brickyards, the ninth plague, and the Red Sea parting

How these are built

One HTML file per game. Vanilla JavaScript, Canvas 2D, zero dependencies, no build step. You can download any of them and double-click it — no server, no install, works offline. Build for repair, not forever.

Pass criteria are locked before a rung is built, then verified by running the thing rather than asserting it. Then a human plays it, because on rung 2 the automated suite passed everything and a five-minute playtest found six real defects it structurally could not catch.

The failures stay in

Each rung keeps a RUN_LOG.md with the wrong turns, false alarms and dead ends left in on purpose. That's not modesty — the mistakes are the teaching material, and a log tidied afterwards is a press release.

Homage to a father who taught “guess and click” — hands on first, theory second.
