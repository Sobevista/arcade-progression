### 2026-07-18 — playtest 3: the rack-reset bug

- Daniel: *"so when died on first level entire aliens reset."* **Real, and the worst bug of the build** — it silently deletes the core tension of the game. Grind the rack down, die once, everything you killed comes back.
- **Cause, and it's structural:** `resetWave(hard)` was called from two different lifecycle events — new wave, and respawn-after-death. The boolean only controlled shield rebuilding, so the respawn path inherited *everything else*, including `makeInvaders()`. I reused the function because it was convenient and never re-examined what the second caller was actually inheriting. Became **INV-11**. [VERIFIED]
- **Fix:** split into `resetWave()` (fresh rack: invaders, shields, march state, UFO timer) and `respawn()` (ship + projectiles only), sharing a small `clearShot()` helper. No boolean.
- **Verified at the state transitions rather than on a timer:** 35 alive before death → 35 after; invader positions byte-identical across the death; invaders correctly frozen during the death animation; bunkers keep their damage; lives decrement; score and wave preserved; ship recentred; bombs cleared at respawn and resuming normally afterwards. Clearing the rack still advances the wave, refills to 55, starts lower, and repairs shields. Game over still fires on the last life. 61 fps. [VERIFIED]
- **Two more false alarms in that run** (positions "not preserved", bombs "not cleared") — both were my test sampling on a timer while the game kept playing, instead of sampling at the state transition. Re-tested at the transitions: both clean. **INV-3 earning its keep for the third time today.**
- Also this round: cut "RUNG 2 OF THE PROGRESSION" and the duplicate ESC hint from the title (UX-11 re-pick trigger fired — kids are about to play it, and internal vocabulary means nothing to a player). Both were the *same* failure — one fact, two homes — which is already law in the harness and which I broke twice on a single screen.
- `README.md` written: run it by double-clicking, four sharing paths cheapest-first, which knobs to poke and which not to. localStorage wrapped in try/catch so opening the file from disk can never crash it — verified by forcing `localStorage` to throw; game keeps running, just doesn't persist the high score.
